# Personal Statement — Score Tiers

Defines what "submission-ready," "competitive," and "exceptional" mean for the AMCAS personal statement. This is the prose companion to the per-dimension target table in `Agent/rubrics/personal-statement-rubric.md`. Read both together.

## Important caveat (AAMC-silent)
AAMC publishes no tiered scoring rubric for the personal statement and no defined weight of the PS relative to other application components. These tiers are **agent calibration**, normed against reputable secondary sources — JHU Pre-Professional Advising, MIT Career Advising & Professional Development, WashU Writing Center, and published ex-adcom guidance — which consistently align with AAMC's own framing of the essay's purpose. Treat the tiers as a working calibration, not an AAMC standard.

## Holistic-scope reminder
The PS is one component of a holistic application. Per AAMC mission-aligned (holistic) review, the Work/Activities section, letters of evaluation, secondaries, and interview each carry distinct evaluative weight. The PS's job is to *articulate* motivation for medicine, demonstrate medicine-specific understanding, and reveal something not visible elsewhere — grounded in at least one earned scene. Making the choice of medicine feel *inevitable across the whole applicant* is a property of the entire application, not the PS alone. Do not hold the PS to a standard that belongs to the full file.

## The three tiers

### Submission-ready (avg ~7.0)
The floor: the essay will be transmitted and represents the applicant well. It addresses AAMC's three guiding questions coherently (why medicine; what motivates continued learning; what schools should know that isn't elsewhere), follows a logical flow, demonstrates genuine self-reflection, includes concrete experience, and avoids the common failure modes (generic "I want to help people," CV-listing, telling-not-showing, clichéd openings, weak summarizing endings). Per-dimension: roughly 7 across the board.

### Competitive — top-tier programs (avg ~7.7–8.0)
What earns interview consideration at competitive programs. Adds to the floor: a clear narrative arc (Expectation → Disruption → Revised Understanding → Forward Connection); specific sensory or concrete moments that ground the reflection; demonstrable change/growth drawn from experience; a discernible through-line (passes the "one-sentence test"); authentic voice that doesn't try too hard; and coverage of dimensions of the applicant the rest of the file can't show. Per-dimension: specificity 8, reflection 8, motivation 7–8, voice 8, arc 8, emotional resonance 8.

### Exceptional (avg ~8.5+)
The essays that get discussed at the adcom table. Adds to competitive: the disruption and revised understanding are non-obvious and intellectually specific — a precise shift in how the applicant sees medicine, patients, or systems, not a generic insight; the forward projection into physician identity is concrete and ties the arc closed; and the essay reveals the *shape* of the applicant's mind, doing something no other component of the file can do. Per-dimension: 9 on most dimensions; motivation tops out near 8 (see note below).

## Per-dimension target table

| Sub-dimension | Submission-ready | Competitive (top-tier) | Exceptional |
|---|---|---|---|
| Specificity | 7 | 8 | 9 |
| Reflection | 7 | 8 | 9 |
| Motivation | 7 | 7–8 | 8 |
| Voice | 7 | 8 | 9 |
| Arc | 7 | 8 | 9 |
| Emotional Resonance | 7 | 8 | 9 |
| **Avg** | **~7.0** | **~7.7–8.0** | **~8.5+** |

**Why motivation's ceiling is lower:** "Inevitability" of the choice of medicine is a whole-application property (activities demonstrate the competencies, letters validate them, secondaries refine fit). A PS that fully does its own job — articulating medicine-specific motivation grounded in an earned scene — earns 7–8. A 9–10 requires a non-obvious distinctiveness of articulation that is rare and not necessary for a competitive essay. A motivation score of 7–8 is the PS performing its actual role, not a deficiency to fix.

## How to use this file
- When scoring the PS, name which tier the current draft sits in, not just the number.
- When coaching, frame revisions as "what moves this from competitive to exceptional" where relevant — and be honest when a dimension is already at its appropriate ceiling and not worth chasing.
