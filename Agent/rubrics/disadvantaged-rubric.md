# Disadvantaged / Other Impactful Essay Rubric

The AMCAS "disadvantaged" / additional-context essay. Conveys adversity and its effect without
self-pity or excuse-making.

**Scored dimensions:** the shared dimensions from [`essay-base-rubric.md`](./essay-base-rubric.md)
— **Specificity, Reflection Depth, Impact Clarity, Voice/Authenticity** — plus the overlay below.
Score each 1–10 with text-anchored reasoning.

> De-personalized: refer to "the applicant," never a name.

---

## Overlay — Adversity Framing

Does it convey real disadvantage and growth without victimhood or blaming, and connect to resilience
relevant to medicine?

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | Either no real adversity shown, or pure complaint/excuse with no growth. |
| 3–4 | Adversity stated; framing leans on blame or asks for sympathy. |
| 5–6 | Genuine adversity + some growth, but framing wobbles between insight and grievance. |
| 7–8 | Honest about hardship, centered on agency and growth; no self-pity. |
| 9–10 | Adversity rendered with dignity and specificity; growth is earned and clearly informs the applicant's path to medicine. |

---

## Scoring checklist
1. Score the 4 base dims + Adversity Framing.
2. avg_score = mean of the 5.
3. Write prose to `feedback/disadvantaged.md`.
