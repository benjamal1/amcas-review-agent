# Personal Statement Rubric

AMCAS Personal Comments Essay: **5,300 characters** max.
AAMC guidance: Why medicine? What motivates learning? What should schools know not disclosed elsewhere? Hardships, grade fluctuations.

Use this rubric when scoring the personal statement. Score each sub-dimension 1–10. Provide 1–2 sentences of text-anchored reasoning for each score.

---

## Sub-dimension 1: Specificity

Does the essay use concrete, verifiable scenes — specific patients, specific moments, specific lab results — rather than generalizations?

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | Entirely generic. Every sentence could apply to any applicant. "I've always wanted to help people." |
| 3–4 | Occasional specifics buried in generalization. One named experience but described in summary form. |
| 5–6 | Mix of specific and generic. Some vivid scenes, some paragraphs that drift into abstraction. |
| 7–8 | Mostly specific. Scenes are named, dated, sensory. Generic claims are rare and minor. |
| 9–10 | Every claim is grounded. Scenes are so specific they could only have been written by this applicant. |

---

## Sub-dimension 2: Reflection Depth

Does the essay show what the experience *taught* the applicant and how their thinking changed — not just what they *did*?

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | No reflection. Pure narration of events. "I did X, then I did Y." |
| 3–4 | Surface reflection. "This taught me medicine is important." Generic takeaway. |
| 5–6 | Some genuine insight but not consistently developed. Reflection appears in some paragraphs, absent in others. |
| 7–8 | Clear evidence of changed thinking. Applicant articulates specifically how a belief or approach shifted. |
| 9–10 | Deep, earned reflection. Applicant traces a non-obvious connection between experience and professional identity. Shows intellectual and emotional honesty. |

---

## Sub-dimension 3: Motivation Clarity

Is the "why medicine" thread present, clear, and earned — not claimed abstractly?

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | No clear "why medicine." Could be a statement for any health profession or career. |
| 3–4 | Motivation stated but not demonstrated. "I want to help people" without evidence of what that means specifically. |
| 5–6 | Motivation present and tied to some experiences, but thread is not consistent throughout. |
| 7–8 | Medicine-specific motivation. Demonstrates understanding of what medicine is (not just "helping") and why specifically medicine, not nursing, research, or public health. |
| 9–10 | Motivation feels inevitable. The essay builds to a conclusion about medicine that feels earned by the evidence. Reader understands why this person and this profession. |

---

## Sub-dimension 4: Voice / Authenticity

Could this essay only have been written by Benjamin? Or could it be swapped with another applicant's essay?

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | Completely generic. Could belong to any applicant. No personality visible. Possibly AI-sounding. |
| 3–4 | Faint personality. A few distinctive moments but largely forgettable phrasing. |
| 5–6 | Recognizable voice in parts. Some memorable phrasing but inconsistent. |
| 7–8 | Distinctive throughout. Reader gets a clear sense of who this person is beyond their resume. |
| 9–10 | Unmistakably Benjamin. Specific enough in thought and detail that it could not be reassigned to another applicant. |

---

## Sub-dimension 5: Narrative Arc

Does the essay have a clear structural progression — a beginning that draws the reader in, a development that builds, and a forward-looking close that connects to medicine?

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | No structure. Chronological list of experiences. Reads like an annotated CV. |
| 3–4 | Beginning and end present but mechanical. "Ever since I was young… I know medicine is my calling." |
| 5–6 | Recognizable structure but not earned. The close doesn't follow naturally from the opening. |
| 7–8 | Clear arc. Opening scene connects to close. Reader feels they've gone on a journey. |
| 9–10 | Elegant structure. Opening and close are in conversation. Essay feels complete and purposeful. Theme is present throughout without being stated explicitly. |

---

## Scoring Checklist

After scoring all five sub-dimensions:
1. Calculate avg_score: (specificity + reflection + motivation + voice + arc) / 5
2. Write reasoning for each score — 1–2 sentences, cite specific text
3. Update `Personal Statement 2026/ps-scores.md` frontmatter
4. Update `Agent/scorecard.md` ps_avg field
5. Recalculate composite
6. Run improvement priorities update
