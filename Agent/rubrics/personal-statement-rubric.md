# Personal Statement Rubric

AMCAS Personal Comments Essay: **5,300 characters** max.
AAMC guidance: Why medicine? What motivates learning? What should schools know not disclosed
elsewhere? Hardships, grade fluctuations.

**Scored dimensions:** the shared dimensions from [`essay-base-rubric.md`](./essay-base-rubric.md)
— **Specificity, Reflection Depth, Voice/Authenticity** — plus the two PS overlays below. Score
each 1–10 with text-anchored reasoning. (Impact Clarity from the base is folded into Motivation +
Arc for the PS.)

> De-personalized: refer to "the applicant," never a name.

---

## Overlay 1 — Motivation Clarity (why medicine)

Is the "why medicine" thread present, clear, and earned — not claimed abstractly?

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | No clear "why medicine." Could be any health profession or career. |
| 3–4 | Motivation stated, not demonstrated ("I want to help people"). |
| 5–6 | Motivation tied to some experiences but inconsistent throughout. |
| 7–8 | Medicine-specific: shows what medicine *is* and why it, not nursing/research/public health. |
| 9–10 | Motivation feels inevitable — the essay builds to a conclusion about medicine earned by the evidence. |

---

## Overlay 2 — Narrative Arc

A beginning that draws in, a development that builds, a forward-looking close that connects to medicine.

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | No structure. Chronological list; reads like an annotated CV. |
| 3–4 | Beginning and end present but mechanical ("Ever since I was young…"). |
| 5–6 | Recognizable structure but the close doesn't follow from the opening. |
| 7–8 | Clear arc — opening scene connects to close; reader feels a journey. |
| 9–10 | Elegant — opening and close in conversation; theme present throughout without being stated. |

---

## Scoring checklist
1. Score: Specificity, Reflection Depth, Voice (base) + Motivation, Narrative Arc (overlays).
2. avg_score = mean of the 5.
3. Write the score + prose to `data.json` (`component_scores.personal_statement`) and
   `feedback/personal-statement.md` — never to legacy `*-scores.md`.
4. Recalculate composite; refresh priorities.
