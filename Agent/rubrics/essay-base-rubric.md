# Essay Base Rubric

Shared scoring dimensions for **all** AMCAS narrative essays (personal statement, impactful
experience, secondaries, disadvantaged/other). Each essay-specific rubric references this base and
adds its own overlay dimension(s). Score each applicable dimension 1–10 with 1–2 sentences of
text-anchored reasoning.

> De-personalized: always refer to "the applicant," never a name.

---

## Base 1 — Specificity

Concrete, verifiable scenes (named people, moments, places, results) vs. generalization.

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | Entirely generic. Every sentence could apply to any applicant ("I've always wanted to help people"). |
| 3–4 | Occasional specifics buried in generalization; one named experience described in summary. |
| 5–6 | Mix of specific and generic — some vivid scenes, some drift into abstraction. |
| 7–8 | Mostly specific. Scenes are named, dated, sensory. Generic claims rare and minor. |
| 9–10 | Every claim grounded. Scenes so specific they could only have been written by this applicant. |

---

## Base 2 — Reflection Depth

What the experience *taught* and how thinking changed — not just what was *done*.

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | No reflection. Pure narration ("I did X, then Y"). |
| 3–4 | Surface reflection ("this taught me medicine is important"). Generic takeaway. |
| 5–6 | Some genuine insight, inconsistently developed. |
| 7–8 | Clear changed thinking; applicant articulates how a belief or approach shifted. |
| 9–10 | Deep, earned reflection tracing a non-obvious connection between experience and identity; intellectual + emotional honesty. |

---

## Base 3 — Impact Clarity

Is it unambiguous *why* the experience/idea mattered and what the actual effect was?

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | No discernible point. Reader can't say why this is in the essay. |
| 3–4 | Impact implied but never stated; reader has to infer. |
| 5–6 | Impact stated but vague or generic. |
| 7–8 | Clear impact — reader could summarize it in one sentence. |
| 9–10 | Impact is sharp, specific, and consequential; reshapes how the reader sees the applicant. |

---

## Base 4 — Voice / Authenticity

Could this only have been written by this applicant, or could it be swapped with anyone's?

| Score | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | Completely generic; no personality. Possibly AI-sounding. |
| 3–4 | Faint personality; a few distinctive moments, largely forgettable phrasing. |
| 5–6 | Recognizable voice in parts; memorable phrasing but inconsistent. |
| 7–8 | Distinctive throughout; clear sense of the person beyond the resume. |
| 9–10 | Unmistakable. Specific enough in thought and detail it could not be reassigned to another applicant. |
