# AAMC Competency Coverage Rubric

Score each of the 17 AAMC Core Competencies **1–10** based on how well the full application (personal statement + activities + rec letters) demonstrates that competency. For each competency, identify which specific application pieces are the supporting evidence.

**Scoring scale:**
- **9–10 (Strong):** Multiple application pieces demonstrate this with specific, vivid evidence. Adcom would notice it.
- **5–8 (Present):** Competency is demonstrated but could be stronger or more explicit.
- **1–4 (Thin):** Barely demonstrated or only abstractly claimed without evidence.
- **0 (Unscored):** Not yet scored.

---

## Professional Competencies (11)

### 1. Commitment to Learning and Growth
*Demonstrates ongoing interest in learning, self-improvement, intellectual curiosity beyond what's required.*

Evidence to look for: Self-directed learning, seeking feedback, returning to master something difficult, teaching others to deepen own understanding, intellectual curiosity in descriptions.

---

### 2. Cultural Awareness
*Recognizes how culture shapes health beliefs and healthcare interactions.*

Evidence to look for: Work with diverse populations, reflection on how patient background affects care, clinical or volunteer settings with underserved communities.

---

### 3. Cultural Humility
*Acknowledges limits of own cultural knowledge, remains open to learning from others.*

Evidence to look for: Moments of recognizing own assumptions, openness to perspectives different from own, non-judgmental engagement with patients or communities.

---

### 4. Empathy and Compassion
*Genuine concern for others' wellbeing, active listening, present with patients and people in difficulty.*

Evidence to look for: Specific patient interactions, moments of genuine emotional engagement, going beyond task to connect with a person.

---

### 5. Ethical Responsibility to Self and Others
*Integrity, honesty, awareness of ethical obligations, acting responsibly when unobserved.*

Evidence to look for: Situations requiring integrity, acknowledging mistakes, navigating ethical complexity in clinical/research settings, honest reflection on limitations.

---

### 6. Interpersonal Skills
*Builds relationships effectively, works across differences, navigates conflict constructively.*

Evidence to look for: Team dynamics in lab or clinical settings, working with difficult personalities, cross-cultural interactions, patient/family communication.

---

### 7. Oral Communication
*Speaks clearly, adapts communication to audience, listens actively.*

Evidence to look for: Presentations, teaching, patient education, clinical team communication, public speaking, conference posters.

---

### 8. Reliability and Dependability
*Can be counted on, follows through, shows up consistently.*

Evidence to look for: Long-term commitments (not one-time activities), leadership roles with responsibility, meeting deadlines, taking ownership of tasks in research or clinical settings.

---

### 9. Resilience and Adaptability
*Maintains effectiveness under stress, recovers from setbacks, adjusts to change.*

Evidence to look for: Academic struggles and recovery, failed experiments, unexpected obstacles in research, personal challenges, pivoting approaches.

---

### 10. Service Orientation
*Commitment to serving others, especially underserved. Puts others' needs before personal gain.*

Evidence to look for: Volunteering (especially sustained, not resume-padding), advocacy work, choosing service activities that aren't obviously career-building, community health work.

---

### 11. Teamwork and Collaboration
*Contributes effectively in groups, supports colleagues, understands interdependence.*

Evidence to look for: Group research projects, clinical team roles, lab group dynamics, co-authorship, supporting peers.

---

## Thinking and Reasoning Competencies (4)

### 12. Critical Thinking
*Analyzes evidence, questions assumptions, synthesizes to form well-reasoned conclusions.*

Evidence to look for: Questioning a research hypothesis, noticing a flaw in methodology, asking non-obvious questions, connecting ideas across domains.

---

### 13. Quantitative Reasoning
*Works comfortably with data, statistics, numerical evidence.*

Evidence to look for: Data analysis in research, statistical interpretation, quantitative coursework performance, engineering problem-solving.

---

### 14. Scientific Inquiry
*Understands the scientific method, asks testable questions, interprets experimental results rigorously.*

Evidence to look for: Research contributions, hypothesis generation, experimental design, understanding controls and confounders.

---

### 15. Written Communication
*Writes clearly, accurately, with appropriate style for the context.*

Evidence to look for: Publications, lab reports, research writing, quality of activity descriptions and personal statement itself.

---

## Science Competencies (2)

### 16. Living Systems
*Understands biological principles at molecular, cellular, organismal, and population levels.*

Evidence to look for: Biology/biochemistry/physiology coursework, research on biological systems, understanding of disease mechanisms.

---

### 17. Human Behavior
*Understands psychological, social, and cultural determinants of behavior and health.*

Evidence to look for: Psychology/sociology coursework, social determinants awareness, understanding patient motivations, behavioral health exposure.

---

## Scoring Process

1. Read the full application (PS + activities + any scored rec letters)
2. For each competency, identify all application pieces that provide evidence
3. Score 1–10 based on strength and specificity of evidence
4. Record supporting pieces in `competency-coverage.md`
5. Calculate avg_score: sum of all 17 scores / 17
6. Update `Agent/scorecard.md` competency_avg field
7. Flag any competency scoring ≤4 as "thin" — these are priority gaps
