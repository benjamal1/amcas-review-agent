# Most Meaningful Experience

> Demo draft (~1,150 characters) — AMCAS limit is 1,325. Replace with your own; this is here so the
> app doesn't look empty on first run.

A trauma patient coded twice before the team stabilized her. I had no clinical role — I ran blankets and paged her family — but I watched the attending narrate every step out loud, not for the patient, but so the team stayed synced under pressure. I started copying that into how I handle chaos: say what you're doing, out loud, so people can catch what you miss.

Three weeks later, her daughter found me in the hallway just to say thank you. I hadn't done anything clinical. I'd walked her to the family waiting room and stayed twenty minutes so she wasn't alone.

I began a notebook that night — one line per patient interaction that surprised me. I still add to it. It reframed the job for me: not only the resuscitation, but also being the person who remembers the family in the hallway. That's the piece of medicine I didn't expect to matter most, and it's the closest thing I have to a reason I want this career instead of just liking science.
